# Technical Report: UPDSF v4.4
## Biophysical Mechanisms of Thymine Selection in Prebiotic Environments

### 1. Objective
The UPDSF v4.4 simulation aims to quantify the stability and dominance of Thymine (T) over Uracil (U) under varying hydrothermal conditions. This report analyzes the "Selection Window" that facilitates the transition from the RNA world to the DNA world, supporting the Matter World Hypothesis (MWH).

### 2. Comparative Analysis of Simulation Scenarios

### 3. Biophysical Mechanisms of Thymine Selection

#### 3.1. The Kinetic Filter Effect
In the hydrothermal scenario (76°C, pH 9.5), the simulation demonstrates a "Kinetic Filter." According to the Arrhenius equation, the degradation rate of Uracil increases exponentially. Because the activation energy (Ea) for Thymine degradation is significantly higher, T survives while U is rapidly eliminated. This creates the extreme stability ratio of 124.2x.

#### 3.2. The Deamination Paradox (C to U)
At pH 9.5, the deamination of Cytosine to Uracil is accelerated. In an Uracil-based system, this would result in massive informational noise (mutations). Thymine, characterized by its methyl group (-CH3), acts as a "Clean Digital Signal," allowing the system to distinguish between intended genetic information and chemical noise.

#### 3.3. Lipid-Driven Stabilization
The simulation shows that lipid vesicles act as a physical shield. Due to its higher hydrophobicity, Thymine integrates more effectively into the vesicle membrane. This synergy between chemical stability and physical encapsulation shields the genetic material from proton-catalyzed hydrolysis, especially in the acidic scenario (pH 5.5).

### 4. Conclusion
The results of UPDSF v4.4 indicate that the transition to a DNA-based genome was not random but driven by environmental constraints. The hydrothermal alkaline environment acts as a powerful accelerator, while the acidic environment provides a stable, albeit slower, pathway for genomic preservation.
