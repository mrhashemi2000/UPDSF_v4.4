## UPDSF v4.4: Global Stability Map of Uracil vs. Thymine



### 1. Executive Summary
This expanded analysis extends the UPDSF v4.4 framework to evaluate the chemical competition between Uracil (U) and Thymine (T) across four diverse prebiotic scenarios. While the original report focused on Hydrothermal Vents, this document provides a universal stability map, validating the Matter World Hypothesis (MWH) by demonstrating that the transition from an RNA-world to a DNA-world was an inevitable consequence of environmental thermodynamic constraints.

### 3. Detailed Biophysical Mechanisms

#### 3.1. Hydrothermal Vents (The Alkaline Accelerator)
In high-temperature ($\sim 76^\circ\text{C}$), alkaline (pH 9.5) conditions, the degradation of Uracil is exponentially accelerated. The Kinetic Filter ensures that only Thymine, with its higher activation energy ($E_a$), survives the hydrolytic stress, leading to an extreme stability ratio of 124.2x.

#### 3.2. Ice / Eutectic Environments (The RNA Haven)
In sub-zero temperatures ($-20^\circ\text{C}$), the simulation reveals a reversal of dominance. The reduced thermal energy inhibits the degradation of Uracil, and the phase-separation (eutectic freezing) concentrates monomers. In this scenario, Uracil exhibits a higher synthesis rate, making ice environments the most plausible cradle for the early RNA-world.

#### 3.3. Volcanic Wet-Dry Cycles (The Ratchet Effect)
The "Sawtooth" simulation (T: $45^\circ\text{C} \leftrightarrow 85^\circ\text{C}$) demonstrates the Ratchet Effect:
- Dry Phase: Evaporation drives polymerization, increasing the count of both U and T.
- Wet Phase: Rapid rehydration causes the hydrolysis of Uracil.
- Result: Thymine retains its structural integrity through the cycle, leading to a step-wise accumulation (accumulation $\approx 11$ copies per 100 cycles).

#### 3.4. Tidal Pools (Photo-Kinetic Selection)
Tidal pools introduce UV radiation as a primary stressor.
- Thermodynamics: The stability ratio is $18.5\text{x}$ in favor of Thymine.
- Photo-Degradation:* Uracil's high absorption cross-section for $254\text{nm}$ photons leads to rapid cleavage.

- *Concentration Flux: During low tide, evaporation increases the concentration, but the subsequent high tide flushes out the unstable Uracil-based polymers, while Thymine-based sequences persist due to the C5-Methyl shielding.

### 5. Final Conclusion
The results of UPDSF v4.4 confirm that the transition to a DNA-based genome was not a random biological event but a chemical necessity. While Uracil could have dominated in cold, ice-based environments, the overwhelming stability of Thymine in hydrothermal, volcanic, and UV-exposed environments ensured its selection as the primary digital signal for genetic preservation.
